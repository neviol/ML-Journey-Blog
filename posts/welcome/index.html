What is overfitting? Provide an example.

  • Overfitting is training a model such that it memorizes the data rather than finds the general correlations between the features. Eventually, the model might give perfect predictions on the training data, but when tested on real world inputs, it will fail terribly. A great example for overfitting is a powerful model that predicts housing prices but memorizes the exact adress instead of finding general correlation. This kind of model could do well on the test data, but since it is so powerful it simply memorizes the adress and color of a building and correlates it to the price instead of finding general rules in the data set.

02 Production:

Provide an example of where the bear classification model might work poorly in production, due to structural or style differences in the training data.

  • An app that should supposedly identify bears for users camping in the wilderness could be one example. The data used in production would most likely be significantly different from the training data. Some images might have very poor quality, lighting, be too far away, and possibly not even feature any bears. Apart from that, the use cases for such an app would also be quite limited since it’s likely that only a very small demographic of people would actually go out of their way (and safety) to take a picture of a bear in the wilderness. On the other hand, if deployed and marketed as as a website for wilderness photographers used to verify their existing predictions about the type of bear they photographed, the tool might deem useful.
